Rain lashed the windows of the tiny tea shop, a rhythmic counterpoint to the nervous drumming of Elara's fingers. Outside, the neon glow of Neo-Seoul blurred into a watery mess. Inside, the air hung heavy with the scent of ginger and jasmine. Elara, a young mechanic with grease perpetually staining her overalls, wasn't supposed to be nervous. This was just another delivery. A simple package, retrieved from a shady corner of the black market.
